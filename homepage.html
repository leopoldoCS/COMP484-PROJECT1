Tyler, The Creator, born Tyler Gregory Okonma on March 6, 1991, in Ladera Heights, California, is an American rapper, producer, and clothing designer. He first gained attention as the founder of the hip-hop collective Odd Future in late 2007. Known for his creativity, he produced and rapped on many of the groups projects, quickly standing out for his style. His debut solo album Bastard and Goblin were controversial but showcased his talent. Tyler's music evolved over the years, moving from shock value to more personal, and experimental sounds. Later albums like Wolf 2013, Cherry Bomb 2015, and Flower Boy 2017 shot him even higher into the spotlight. In 2019, his album IGOR won the Grammy Award for Best Rap Album, making his respect as a creator grow. He followed up with Call Me If You Get Lost 2022, which he recieved his second grammy. Tyler is a fashion designer, launching his own clothing brand, Golf Wang and Le Fluer and the annual Camp Flog Gnaw festival. Today, he is one of the most influential artists of his generation, blending music, fashion, and culture in his own original way.
